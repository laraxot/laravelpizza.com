**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Last Updated:** March 12, 2026

---

## Research Goals

1. Understand notification preferences
2. Identify channel preferences
3. Validate notification frequency
4. Determine content preferences

---

## Research Questions

| ID | Question | Priority |
|----|----------|----------|
| RQ1 | Which channels do users prefer? | P0 |
| RQ2 | How often do users want notifications? | P0 |
| RQ3 | What content is valuable? | P1 |
| RQ4 | When should notifications be sent? | P1 |

---

## Methodology

- **Surveys:** 500+ users on notification preferences
- **Interviews:** 20 users on notification experiences
- **A/B Testing:** Timing and content variations
- **Analytics:** Notification engagement patterns

---

## Participant Profiles

### Heavy User
- **Characteristics:** Highly engaged
- **Preferences:** Real-time, all channels
- **Concerns:** Missing important updates

### Casual User
- **Characteristics:** Moderate engagement
- **Preferences:** Digest, email only
- **Concerns:** Too many notifications

### Minimal User
- **Characteristics:** Low engagement
- **Preferences:** Critical only
- **Concerns:** Notification fatigue

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Channel Preferences Vary
Different users prefer different channels.

### Finding 2: Timing Matters
Notifications at wrong time get ignored.

### Finding 3: Control Valued
Users want notification preferences.

### Finding 4: Relevance Critical
Irrelevant notifications lead to opt-out.

---

## Recommendations

### Immediate
- Implement preference center
- Add notification batching
- Create valuable content

### Long-Term
- Build smart timing
- Develop personalization
- Create automation workflows
